Lean Six Sigma Project

Reducing Audit Rebuttal Rate in AR Quality - RCM

Department: Quality Assurance

Prepared By: Raman (Role: Project Lead)

Duration: April 2022 - June 2022

Executive Summary

This project aimed to reduce the AR audit rebuttal rate from 13% to below 5% within 3 months to increase audit consistency, reduce rework and cycle time, and strengthen stakeholder confidence in Quality Assurance.

Define Phase

Objective: Reduce reiterations and improve audit accuracy for denial resolution and claims processing.

Scope: AR quality audits for denial resolution and claims processing across client Lines of Business, including QA workflows, calibration, SOP alignment, and feedback communication.

Approach: DMAIC methodology following Lean Six Sigma principles.

Measure Phase

Collected baseline data on audit rebuttal rates and rework instances. Established key metrics such as rebuttal percentage, audit cycle time, and audit consistency scores.

Analyze Phase

Performed root cause analysis identifying issues in SOP deviations, feedback communication lapses, and calibration inconsistencies.

Improve Phase

Implemented revised SOPs, enhanced communication protocols, and conducted targeted training for auditors aligned with new standards.

Control Phase

Established control charts, ongoing monitoring and periodic calibration reviews, resulting in sustained improvement and process stability.

Results and Conclusion

Metric Before Improvement After Improvement
Audit Rebuttal Rate 13% 4.5%
Cycle Time Reduction 7 days 3.5 days
Audit Consistency Score 72% 90%

The project achieved the objective with significant reductions in audit rebuttal rates and cycle times, increasing overall quality assurance rigor and stakeholder confidence.
